Introduction

Charts have become central analytical interfaces for discovery and decision support, but most multimodal systems still solve shallow tasks such as chart QA and factual lookup. The core challenge is chart deep research: integrating background knowledge, precise fact extraction, relationship construction, deep report synthesis, and forecasting into a coherent analytical pipeline.

We identify two bottlenecks: a training bottleneck from reward interference and heterogeneous data optimization conflicts, and an evaluation bottleneck from subjective, hard-to-scale open-ended assessment. PRPO and MCDR-Bench are designed to resolve these two bottlenecks jointly.

Abstract

We target chart deep research, where models must go beyond visual QA and produce reliable analytical reasoning. PRPO performs parallel optimization across reward dimensions and capability-aware data partitions, reducing optimization conflicts in RL post-training. MCDR-Bench converts subjective report generation into objective error identification via controlled error injection. Together, they provide a unified framework for training and evaluating deep chart reasoning.

PRPO decomposes optimization across reward dimensions and data types, improving exploration and reducing optimization conflicts.

Method

PRPO addresses both optimization conflict sources: interference across reward dimensions and imbalance across heterogeneous capability data.

Reward-PRPO

Computes dimension-wise relative advantages instead of scalarized rewards, preserving discriminative optimization signals for different reasoning aspects.

Data-PRPO

Partitions samples by capability type and uses iterative outlier relegation to prevent unstable partition statistics from dominating updates.

Unified PRPO

Combines reward and data parallelization to support balanced capability growth in complex multi-task chart reasoning.

PRPO demonstrates better early exploration and stronger late-stage stability than GRPO, consistent with reduced reward and data-level optimization conflicts.

MCDR-Bench

MCDR-Bench is designed around the error uniqueness principle. It transforms subjective report quality judgment into objective error identification over five dimensions: Background Knowledge (BG), Fact Extraction (FE), Relationship Construction (RL), Deep Research (DR), and Forecast/Plan (F/P).

It follows a two-phase protocol: (1) multi-agent deep report generation over complex charts, and (2) targeted error injection to build fine-grained objective evaluation tasks.
