ICLR 2026 Submission

Chart Deep Research in LVLMs via Parallel Relative Policy Optimization

PRPO introduces parallel optimization across reward dimensions and capability partitions, while MCDR-Bench establishes objective deep-research evaluation through controllable error injection.

Main Results BibTeX Code

Abstract

We target chart deep research, where models must go beyond visual QA to produce high-level analytical reasoning and planning. Existing post-training methods suffer from multi-dimensional reward interference and heterogeneous data optimization conflicts. Existing benchmarks also under-evaluate deep analytical ability. We propose PRPO for stable parallel preference optimization and MCDR-Bench for objective capability evaluation. Together, they establish a unified framework for training and assessment of chart deep research in LVLMs.

PRPO decomposes optimization across reward dimensions and data types, improving exploration and reducing optimization conflicts.

Method

Reward-PRPO

Computes dimension-specific advantages instead of collapsing heterogeneous rewards to one scalar, preserving optimization signal diversity.

Data-PRPO

Partitions training data by capability type and applies iterative outlier relegation to avoid unstable group statistics.

Unified PRPO

Combines reward parallelization and data parallelization to mitigate both reward interference and heterogeneous optimization conflicts.

MCDR-Bench

MCDR-Bench is built on the error uniqueness principle. It transforms subjective open-ended report generation into objective error identification across five dimensions: Background Knowledge, Fact Extraction, Relationship Construction, Deep Report, and Forecast/Plan.
